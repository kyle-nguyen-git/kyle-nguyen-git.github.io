About Me

Human Biology • Research • Building things

I’m Kyle Nguyen, a Human Biology student at UT Austin interested in computational biology, genomics, and translational research. I like projects that sit at the intersection of biology + data + real-world impact— especially work that turns messy biological questions into clean, reproducible analysis pipelines.

What I’m focused on

  • Computational biology: sequence analysis, peptide/neoantigen pipelines, genomics-oriented data workflows
  • Translational research: projects that connect computation to immunology, disease mechanisms, and therapies
  • Building: structured workflows, documentation, and tools that make projects easier to extend and hand off

Experience snapshot

  • NYU Grossman School of Medicine: computational pipeline work for cancer neoantigen discovery
  • UTMB: exosome / viral infection project with expression-focused analysis and poster work
  • Clinical: EMT experience and clinical exposure that keeps my research interests grounded
