UTMB Research

During my time at UTMB, I worked in a translational research setting using computational analysis to study how viral infection can shift molecular signals carried in exosomes. My work focused on organizing and analyzing biological datasets, validating patterns in expression changes, and communicating results through figures and presentations.

Open poster PDF

Abstract

Exosomes are extracellular vesicles that carry biologically active cargo and can reflect how cells respond to infection and stress. In this project, we examined how viral infection influences exosome-associated molecular signals by comparing infected and control conditions and evaluating shifts in expression patterns. Using computational processing and analysis workflows, we generated a curated set of differentially expressed features and summarized results through visualizations to support downstream interpretation in a translational context.
